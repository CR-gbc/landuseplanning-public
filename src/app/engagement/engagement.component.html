Public Engagement

Engagement with local governments, the public, industry, non-government organizations, and other stakeholders is fundamental to modernized land use planning to ensure regional socio-economic and environmental issues are identified in all active planning areas.

Pre-Planning

Land management issues are discussed between the B.C. government and Indigenous government(s), as well as with stakeholders and communities. Resource managers try to resolve issues using existing land use plans or other stewardship initiatives. If there are no existing land use plans for the area, or the existing land use plans are no longer an effective tool to resolve land use issues, the B.C. government and Indigenous government(s) will determine an appropriate path forward. If land use planning is the desired approach, the parties will begin scoping out a proposed land use planning project, including timelines and costs, then seek a mandate from the Minister of Forests, Lands, Natural Resource Operations and Rural Development to secure resources and proceed.

  • Key Components/ Outcomes
    • Agreement between B.C. government and Indigenous government(s) to pursue land use planning
    • Seek mandate from the Minister of Forests, Lands, Natural Resource Operations and Rural Development
