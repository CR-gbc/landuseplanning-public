The purpose of land use planning is to set strategic direction to guide sustainable resource stewardship and management of provincial public land and waters. Land use plans are considered when reviewing proposed land use activities. Resource management objectives – statements of desired outcomes for resource values or resource use – are an important part of land use plans and are in some cases legally established to support the implementation of plans or agreements.

Direction generated by planning processes is implemented by the B.C. government using a combination of provincial legislation, regulations, ministerial orders, and orders-in-council. In general, these tools require Cabinet or a Minister of a specific Ministry (or a Minister’s delegate) to establish land use designations and resource management objectives.

Examples of applicable legislation include, but are not limited to, the:

  • Land Act
  • Forest and Range Practices Act
  • Oil and Gas Activities Act
  • Wildlife Act
  • Sustainable Water Act
  • Parks Act
  • Environment and Land Use Act
  • Ecological Reserve Act
