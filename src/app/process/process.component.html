Land Use Planning Process & Phases

Land use planning projects in British Columbia will vary in complexity, scope, and timelines. However, there are common steps that are required to complete a land use plan, and have it approved and implemented.

Steps for Completing a Land Use Plan

  1. Pre-Planning. Collaborate with First Nations and agree in principle to undertake a land use plan to resolve land-based issues. Develop a business case and seek mandates to proceed.
  2. Plan Initiation. Get organized to conduct a planning process. Formalize agreement to proceed with planning, finalize planning scope, develop a project budget and stakeholder engagement strategy.
  3. Plan Development. Apply information and planning tools to produce scenario analyses that address planning issues. Select a preferred planning scenario and prepare a draft plan.
  4. Plan Evaluation and Approval. Evaluate draft land use plan, provide dispute resolution and revise plan as required. Submit land use plan for review and approval by planning partners.
